Dishanta Kpatrick

Frontend Engineer at Intuit Mailchimp

Introduction

Dishanta Kpatrick exemplifies leadership in advancing equity for marginalized groups in tech

Her journey, from self-taught coder to front-end engineer, highlights her commitment to dismantling systemic barriers for people of color, women, and non-traditional career seekers.

Championing Representation

As a Black woman in tech, Kpatrick leverages her platform to amplify representation. At Intuit Mailchimp’s Atlanta office, she thrives in an environment where Black leadership is prioritized. For example, three subsequent levels of management are led by Black women.

Kpatrick actively engages in employee resource groups (ERGs) for people of color and attends conferences like AFROTECH to advocate for visibility in an industry where Black professionals remain underrepresented.

Advocating Accessible Pathways

Now a mentor herself, Kpatrick promotes apprenticeship programs to reduce reliance on costly boot camps or degrees. She also encourages freelancing or targeting non-tech companies for entry-level roles.

She guides others, stressing the importance of self-advocacy and portfolio-building. Her work on Mailchimp’s analytics tools, which empower small businesses to track marketing performance, directly supports economic equity for underrepresented entrepreneurs.

“Every experience counts—especially for those shut out of traditional pipelines.”
"Teach yourself as much as you can, the resources are there. Build that portfolio and then go find the work, it’s there."

Ethan Tu

Founder of Taiwan AI Labs

Introduction

Ethan Tu was once a reasearcher and developer in AI for Microsoft.

He would leave Microsoft and in 2017, he founded Taiwan AI Labs, a lab that collaborates with the government and other academias to develop AI.

Instead of creating something similar to ChatGPT, he and Taiwan AI Labs decided to develop their own AI model that better suited the domestic needs of Taiwan.

Developing a Different Kind of AI

Instead of creating another version of ChatGPT, he and his team at Taiwan AI Labs decided to be different. They would develop an AI model that better suited the needs of Taiwan, one that focused on healthcare, smart city, and human interaction.

Due to the rising pandemic of COVID-19 at the time, the lab developed and used the AI to detect COVID-19 in chest X-rays, as well as drug screening and building genome sequencing platform.

Along with the use in healthcare, the labs AI, "Yating", can also detect fake news and identify the original source.

Representing a Nation

As a Taiwanese, Ethan has not only helped so many, but he has also inspired many people both in the US as well as Taiwan.

He has advocated for the combination of Taiwan technology advatage and applying it to the US market's big data to many young Taiwanese engineers.

Along with the US, he firmly believes he can change the industry in Taiwan as he wants to be a model for others to flip the software industry in Taiwan to something that can foster an environment to grow talent.
